Japanese Riichi Mahjong Guide - v1.0 (2026)

By Christopher Yee

A step-by-step guide for beginners to learn and play Japanese Riichi Mahjong

About This Guide

This project began as a small collection of reference sheets I printed for friends and family while teaching them Japanese Riichi Mahjong. Over time, those sheets grew into a more comprehensive set of materials that I wanted to share more broadly.

I wrote with the hope to create a clear, modern resource that helps learners understand the game step by step. The guide features a structured curriculum of readings and practice activities so learning and playing occur simultaneously, with reference sheets designed to support both learning and teaching the game.

My inspiration came both from my experiences in educational instruction and from how I originally learned to play from my grandmother. We played without scoring, additional rules, or special equipment, simply trying to make four sets and a pair with our tiles and discovering the patterns as we went.

My goal is to streamline the learning process for newcomers to Riichi, while also covering the full rules once learners are comfortable with the basics.

Although the packets are designed to be used together as a complete curriculum, they work just as individual reference materials if you prefer. At the end of the day, all you really need is a set of tiles.

Whether you’re learning with friends or teaching others, this guide is meant to make the game approachable, fun, and rewarding — providing a clear path to learn and enjoy the game for years to come.
